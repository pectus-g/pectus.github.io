Yoru

Eclipse of Tails

A narrative action-adventure about judging lost spirits in a Japanese purgatory, as a cat who used to be a grandmother. An old woman named Tomoe and her granddaughter's cat Pera died together during an eclipse. In purgatory, they woke up as one being: Yoru, a two-tailed Nekomata with no memory of who she was. Ten yōkai bosses from real Japanese folklore are trapped here. You decide what happens to each one.

Discover the Tale

Echoing Tales Between Worlds

You'll meet ten yōkai across the game. Oni, Kitsune, Yuki-Onna, Gashadokuro, and others pulled from real Japanese legends. Every one of them used to be a person. Each carries a Kegare, a spiritual pollution, the specific attachment that keeps their soul trapped in purgatory. Your job is to free them, one way or another.

👹

What Makes a Monster?

The Oni didn't start angry. Betrayal did that. The Kitsune's trickery comes from centuries of loneliness. Every boss is rooted in actual Japanese folklore, and every one of them has a reason they ended up here. You'll learn those reasons whether you want to or not.

⚖️

Two Souls, One Body

Yoru isn't one person. Tomoe the grandmother sees emotional auras, reads the grief behind the rage. Pera the cat fights on instinct, fast and aggressive. Switch between them freely. The old woman's wisdom and the cat's power are both yours.

🌑

The Eclipse Awaits

Fill both tails, five rings on each, and the Eclipse triggers. It opens a hidden chapter that recontextualizes everything you've done. Seven endings total. The one you get depends on the exact balance you carried. You can't game it. You have to play it honestly.

Yoru remembers nothing at the start. Each boss you free returns one piece of who Tomoe was. Ten bosses, ten memories. By the final encounter, you know the whole story.

Your Choice

Fight Them or Understand Them

Every boss encounter starts the same way: a spirit in pain, a story you don't know yet. What you do next is up to you. Combat breaks the attachment keeping them trapped. Persuasion untangles it. Both free the soul, but only one reveals the full story of who they were.

Combat

Fight yōkai head-on as Yoru the cat spirit. Dodge, parry, combo. Each defeated boss drops a dark soul, a burning ring on your left tail. The world darkens around you: fog rolls in, spirit lanterns ignite, the air gets heavy. Your left tail grows stronger. You can summon the spirits you've killed through necromancy, because Nekomata in Japanese folklore have always had that power. Faster, more aggressive, but you'll never hear the boss's full story.

Echo Walk

Transform into Tomoe, the old woman Yoru used to be. In human form, you can see emotional auras that the cat can't. Approach the boss and enter the Echo Walk: you physically walk through their floating memories in the arena, piecing together what happened to them in the right emotional order. Get it wrong three times and you'll have to fight. Get it right, and the boss finds peace. A glowing ring on your right tail. The world warms, fog lifts, moonlight breaks through, autumn colours deepen.

Ten bosses. Ten rings across two tails. Every ring you earn is visible on Yoru's body, and every spirit in purgatory can see what kind of judge you've been. Fill both tails to five and the Eclipse triggers: a hidden final chapter with seven possible endings, each one shaped by the exact balance you carried.

A World That Watches

The Sky Tells You Who You've Been

There's no morality bar. The world itself tracks your choices across twenty-nine atmospheric states: time of day, weather, fog density, lighting, even how spirits react when they see you. Lean dark and twilight descends. Lean light and the world opens up. You don't need a UI element to know where you stand. Just look around.
